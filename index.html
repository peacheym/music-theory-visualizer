Music Theory Visualization

This visulaization framework intends to be a simple to use method for understanding basic music theory concepts such as notes, chords, keys and chord-progressions.

How to use:

  • Piano Keys - This visualization is "read only" meaning that while it provides meaningful information, you do not need to interact with it directly.
  • Key Visualization - This visualization shows the structure of Keys based on a specific root note. A breakdown of the color reasoning is as follows.
    • Blue & Large → Major Chord
    • Gray & Small → Minor Chord
    • Red & Large → Diminished Chord
  • Notes Visualization - This visulaization shows all possible notes in a single octave of 12TET music. When you click on a note, you will be selecting it as the root note for the rest of the visualizations.
      • Green & Large → Notes associated with current selection
      Green & Small → Notes not associated with current selection but associated with root note
      Purple & Small → Notes not associated with current selection or root note

  • Chord Progression Selector - A list of all supported chord progressions. A new selection will update the chord-progression visualization.
  • Chord Type Selector - A list of all supported chord types. A new selection will update the notes and piano keys visualizations.
